Mission

Who We Are

The GeoDesign + Planning Lab at the University of Texas at San Antonio works to promote sustainable and resilient communities through geospatial research approaches to urban design and planning processes.

We focus on measuring geographic context, land use patterns, and environmental conditions using GIS and spatial modeling to identify patterns and policy recommendations that enhance quality of life in socially vulnerable communities.

Our interdisciplinary team brings together urban planners, designers, and data scientists to address the most pressing challenges facing cities — from rapid urbanization and neighborhood abandonment to climate change adaptation and environmental justice.

Research Focus

Four Core Areas

Our work spans four interconnected domains shaping the health, equity, and resilience of urban communities.

🏙️

Built Environment

Addresses challenges from rapid urbanization affecting human well-being. We study urban forms, structures, vacancies, and walkability — recognizing how environmental design can either worsen or improve neighborhood conditions and promote equity.

🌿

Green Infrastructure

Examines natural resources serving urban infrastructure purposes, analyzing benefits and connections to neighborhood socioeconomic characteristics from an environmental justice perspective. Topics include tree canopy, green spaces, parks, and low-impact development.

🌡️

Climate Adaptation

Analyzes current built and green infrastructure to recommend climate adaptation tools addressing extreme weather events, urban heat, flooding, and disaster mitigation in vulnerable communities.

🤝

Social Vulnerability

Investigates the intersection of environmental conditions and social equity, identifying communities with high tolerance but low resilience and recommending improvements to the social and physical environment.
