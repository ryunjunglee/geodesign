Research Areas

What We Study

Our lab uses GIS and spatial modeling to measure geographic context, land use patterns, and environmental conditions across four core domains.

🏙️

Built Environment

Urban forms, structures, vacancies, and walkability — examining how design shapes neighborhood equity and human well-being.

🌿

Green Infrastructure

Tree canopy, parks, and low-impact development analyzed through an environmental justice lens.

🌡️

Climate Adaptation

Recommending tools to address extreme weather events, heat, flooding, and disaster mitigation in urban areas.

🤝

Social Vulnerability

Identifying patterns and policy recommendations that enhance quality of life in socially vulnerable communities.

Our Mission

Geospatial Research for Equitable Cities

We work to promote sustainable and resilient communities through geospatial research approaches to urban design and planning processes — measuring geographic context, land use patterns, and environmental conditions to identify patterns and policy recommendations that enhance quality of life.
