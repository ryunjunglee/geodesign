About

GeoDesign + Planning for Sustainable & Resilient Communities

Our lab uses GIS and spatial modeling to measure geographic context, land use patterns, and environmental conditions — identifying patterns and policy recommendations that enhance quality of life in socially vulnerable communities.

We bring together urban planners, designers, and data scientists to address the most pressing challenges facing cities today, from rapid urbanization to climate change adaptation.

Research

Core Research Areas

Our research spans four interconnected domains that shape the health, equity, and resilience of urban communities.

🏙️

Built Environment

Addressing challenges from rapid urbanization affecting human well-being — covering urban forms, structures, vacancies, and walkability. We examine how environmental design can worsen or improve neighborhood conditions and promote equity.

🌿

Green Infrastructure

Examining natural resources serving urban infrastructure purposes, analyzing benefits and connections to neighborhood socioeconomic characteristics. Topics include tree canopy, green spaces, parks, and low-impact development.

🌡️

Climate Adaptation

Analyzing current built and green infrastructure to recommend climate adaptation tools addressing extreme weather events, heat, flooding, and disaster mitigation in urban contexts.

🤝

Social Vulnerability

Investigating the intersection of environmental conditions and social equity, with an environmental justice lens focused on historically underserved and socially vulnerable communities.
